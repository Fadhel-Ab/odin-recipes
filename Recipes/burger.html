Description

A classic burger is a hearty and flavorful sandwich featuring a juicy beef patty served between a soft, toasted bun.
Topped with fresh lettuce, ripe tomatoes, crisp onions, pickles, and your favorite condiments like ketchup, mustard, and mayonnaise,
it's a timeless dish loved for its satisfying combination of textures and flavors.
Perfect for a backyard barbecue or a casual meal!

Ingredients

  • Ground beef (80/20 lean-to-fat ratio, for juicy patties)
  • Burger buns
  • Cheese slices (optional, such as cheddar or American cheese)
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Tomato slices
  • Onion slices (raw or caramelized, as preferred)
  • Pickles (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper (to season the beef)
  • Condiments: ketchup, mustard, mayonnaise, or others of your choice

    • Optional additions:

  • Bacon strips

Steps

  1. Prepare the beef patties:
    Divide the ground beef into equal portions and shape them into round patties, slightly larger than the buns.
    Press a small indentation in the center to prevent puffing during cooking.
    Season both sides with salt and pepper.

  2. Cook the patties:
    Heat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat.
    Cook the patties for about 3-4 minutes per side for medium doneness, or adjust the time to your preference.
    Add a slice of cheese on top during the last minute of cooking to let it melt.

  3. Toast the buns: Lightly butter the cut sides of the burger buns and toast them on the grill or in a skillet until golden.

  4. Assemble the burger:
    Start with the bottom bun, then layer it with lettuce, the cooked patty (with melted cheese, if using), tomato slices, onion slices, and pickles.
    Spread your favorite condiments on the top bun and place it over the toppings.
